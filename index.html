The UPRI Internship Program offers students from diverse academic backgrounds, both within and beyond the University, a valuable opportunity to apply the theories and techniques learned in class to real-world challenges in Disaster Risk Reduction and Management (DRRM). Students also benefit from the chance to learn directly from professionals in the field.
Through this program, the UP Resilience Institute (UPRI) reinforces its commitment to strengthening the academic community’s role in integrating and mainstreaming DRRM and Climate Change Adaptation and Mitigation (CCAM) into education, empowering learners to become more resilient to climate and disaster risks.

Undergraduate Internship Program

First launched in 2020 during the midyear term of the UP School Calendar, this program has been open to undergraduates each midyear since then.
The UPRI welcomes undergraduate interns from various UP constituent universities as well as from numerous higher education institutions across the country. Initially conducted online due to the pandemic, the program transitioned to in-person participation starting in 2022.
UPRI’s commitment to a multidisciplinary approach in DRRM and CCAM allows it to host students from a wide array of academic backgrounds.

    NOAH - WebGIS

    What is your preferred mode of participation for your student interns?

    Description of your division/team.
    • WebGIS team consists of people with varying backgrounds all working towards a common goal – develop new technologies to realize the goal of providing accurate and timely data for disaster mitigation in the country.

    • The Education division supports the development of formal academic programs within the UP System on climate change and disaster risk resilience, empowers individuals, organizations, institutions, and communities towards climate change and disaster resilience through non-formal education, and helps strengthen the academic community in their efforts in integrating and mainstreaming DRR and CCA in their educational programs such as teaching, research, and extension services.

    1. Provide one key project or output from your team.
    • UP NOAH Website - NOAH is a comprehensive platform designed to enhance disaster risk reduction and management in the Philippines. It provides near real-time data, weather forecasts, hazard maps, and risk assessments to help communities, local governments, and policymakers prepare for and mitigate the impacts of natural hazards such as floods, landslides, and storm surges. By integrating advanced science and technology with open data, NOAH empowers Filipinos to make informed decisions and foster resilience against disasters.

    •Basics of Resilience Massive Open Online Courses

    2. Provide one key project or output from your team.
    • UP NOAH Mobile Application (Android and iOS)

    • Philippine Academic Society Climate and Disaster Resilience Annual Conference

    3. Provide one key project or output from your team.
    • GINA(Get Indigenous trees to Adopt) - The GINA app revolves around addressing the need of the Philippines for tree planters. The country is already losing approximately 47,000 hectares of forest cover yearly, according to the Department of Environment and Natural Resources. This app gives awareness to the users of the problem and what the users can do to help. The app enables the users to have access to a wide option of tree species and their seeds and give the users the opportunity to plant and share what and where they planted it. The app is also gamified by earning points per seed purchase and per yearly documentation. These points can earn incentives in the future.
